The Process

Since the Act passed in 2012 there has been a lot of progress on the FirstNet project. FirstNet has staffed up - hiring experts in designing, deploying, and operating broadband data network. States, like Maryland, have also been busy. Maryland has used its portion of the state and local implementation grant funds to hire the broadband team, and start the outreach and data collection process.

Consultation

The Act requires that FirstNet consult with states on the design of the national public safety broadband network (NPSBN). This iterative process, often referred to simply as “consultation”, will produce a Radio Access Network Plan for deployment of the NPSBN in Maryland.

On July 29th, Maryland became the first state to begin the formal consultation process. You can read more about that meeting on FirstNet's blog. This initial consultation meeting brought together senior FirstNet leadership and Maryland public safety, I/T, and executive leaders (pictured at right). The MDInterop Team has prepared 13 suggestions/lessons learned to help other states prepare for consultation. Maryland will build on this initial meeting, collecting user requirements, continuing to exchange ideas, requirements, and design proposals with FirstNet as the consultation process continues.

Collecting Data & User Needs

In Maryland process of getting to a final plan for the deployment and operation of the network will involve:

  1. Collecting stakeholder thoughts, ideas, feedback and comments through active outreach;
  2. Turning the data shared by stakeholders into actionable information that can be used to create the plan for FirstNet deployment and operation in Maryland; and finally.
  3. Sharing that data through a process called "consultation" with the federal FirstNet team, so they can work interactively with Maryland to develop a final plan that meets the needs of Maryland's first responders.
